Who We Are

Heart of Gold Medical International (HOGMI) is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to bringing health education, essential medications, and medical care to underserved communities in remote African villages. Our mission is simple: to bring healing, knowledge, and hope to those who would otherwise have little access to medical care.

Through our medical outreach programs, we provide free medical screenings and diagnostics, administer medications through trained medical professionals, and distribute health education materials that help communities understand and manage preventable illnesses.

The Problem

  • Many rural communities lack access to medical care.
  • Conditions like epilepsy are misunderstood and heavily stigmatized.
  • Without health education or treatment, many patients suffer unnecessary disability and early death.

What We Do

  • Free medical screenings and diagnostics
  • Distribution of essential medications
  • Epilepsy treatment and monitoring
  • Quarterly evaluation and medication refills for diabetics and hypertensives
  • Community health education
  • Financial support since the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Food distribution to vulnerable individuals in Ezinnachi
  • Provision of reading glasses

Our Founder

Chidi Nwaohia

Heart of Gold Medical International was founded by Chidi Nwaohia, who was born and raised in Ezinnachi Okigwe Imo State Nigeria, where she started this mission before going to other remote villages in African countries.

By the divine grace of God, Chidi practiced as a registered nurse for over 29 years in both Nigeria and the United States. Through her years of service, she recognized a powerful truth: many lives could be transformed if only access to medications and basic health education were made available.

As the sole survivor of ten siblings, Chidi has never forgotten what God brought her through. She believes deeply that her life was spared for a purpose. She knows that, "To whom much is given, much is expected."

"Love is not just a feeling—it is action. Love is intentional."

Our Focus: Epilepsy Care

The Challenge

In many rural African communities, epilepsy is deeply misunderstood. Because some believe the condition is contagious or spiritual, those living with epilepsy are often isolated, rejected, stigmatized and isolated even by their own parents.

Children are sent out of school as soon as their disease condition is known. Since Chidi started to go to schools and communities educating them that epilepsy is neither contagious nor evil spirits, these kids are now allowed in the schools. Tragically, most of the kids living with epilepsy in these communities do not survive beyond their teens. We have lost many of our patients due to drowning in the streams or falling into fire during seizures, or sheer neglects.

The Solution

Yet epilepsy is a treatable medical condition. With proper medication and consistent care, individuals can live normal and productive lives.

Without knowledge and medical resources, however, many communities remain unable to manage epilepsy and other chronic conditions.

Our Journey

2006 & 2008

Chidi began personally sponsoring medical mission trips, serving in the Bangui, Central African Republic with a Christian organization.

2013 & 2015

In 2013 and 2015, she undertook additional solo mission trips focused specifically on epilepsy and other chronic ailments in Ezinnachi. With the support of volunteers, physicians, and other medical professionals, and local leaders, life-saving medications and medical care were delivered to many in need.

October 2016

The United States Internal Revenue Service officially granted Heart of Gold Medical International 501(c)(3) nonprofit status.

Ongoing Commitment

Epilepsy treatment is lifelong. Patients receive medication refills every three months, and supplies are carefully monitored from the United States to ensure continuity of care. Those who adhere to their treatment plans experience significant reductions in seizures and improved quality of life.


We remain grateful to God for His guidance and provision. We continue to pray for healing, hope, and the resources needed to serve even more communities in need.
